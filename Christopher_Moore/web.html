Greetings,

This website is a quick and easy guide to building a website for the EECBers (faculty and graduate students) at UNR.  There are more general, better sites that are more thorough (e.g. wikiHow) that you can find by searching Google.

I have divided the site into FOUR parts that I think are important for an effective web presence:

    First: Many people throw together piecemeal, but a well thought-out site is preferred.

    Second: There is a multitude of ways to build your site.  I review popular, easy ways.

    Third: What you need to know for uploading your site.

    Fourth: Out-of-date websites are sore to the eyes and should be updated.

Enjoy, and please send me feedback so I can better make this more accessible.


CONTENT

Before creating your website, please consider the content and format that best showcase your work.

Essential webpages on websites created by biologists include:

    Home

    Research

    Publications

    Contact information

Additional pages include:

    Biography.  A page on its own or under the optional People page.

    Photographs.  Usually of their study systems, but includes personal photos too.

    CV.  Probably more important for graduate students than faculty.

    Teaching (courses).  Very common, then you can create your own pages to use.

    Media.  If applicable, a way to show that your research is important to the greater World.

    Resources.  Miscellaneous links (collaborators, etc.), datasets, protocols, etc.

    People.  Usually some information on the lab members.

    Prospective students.  Pertinent information for doing so.

    News.  If you can keep updating your site, this looks great (more below; MAINTENANCE).

Here are some links to a few sites that have good formatting and content in my opinion: Jordi Bascompte, Alex Badyaev, Carla D’Antonio, and Neo Martinez.



BUILDING THE SITE

There are FOUR options here, listed from least to greatest effort:

1.  Hire a professional.  This is not really necessary for most personal webpages.  This is usually important for businesses and other larger organizations.

2.  Acquire a website-building program (e.g., iWeb, Adobe Dreamweaver) and do it yourself.  These programs (especially iWeb) are VERY easy to use.  iWeb is essentially presentation program (Keynote or PowerPoint) with an upload button.  Meaning, if you work well in those programs, these website building programs will be a cinch.

    Note: these programs cost $$$.  If you have a Mac, however, iWeb is free.

3.  Use an online site generator.  There are great websites that allow you to create your webpage online.  Matt Forister, for instance, uses GoogleSites.  Another great site is WordPress, used by 14% of the biggest 1,000,000 websites.

4.  Learn one or more programming languages and build a website from scratch!  Good luck.  If you can do that, you have probably stopped reading this page a long time ago.

*Options 1,2, and 4 allow you to keep your url: www.unr.edu/~username (unr.edu as the domain name).  Option 3 requires you to use their domain name, but you get to personalize a subdomain name (e.g., https://sites.google.com/site/greatbasinbuglab/home/).

**Ranking the above options from most to least customizable: 4, 1, 2, 3.



UPLOADING THE SITE

If you choose to use an online site generator (option 3 above), then you can skip this section and go to MAINTENANCE below because by selecting this option your website will already live in the Internet.

If you chose option 2 from above, then the software will likely have a way for you to ‘one-click’ update.  Similar to having an email client that that has a ‘one-click’ to send and receive messages, you will input some of the information below and will never have to worry about it again.

For options 1 or 4 from above, you will need to download an FTP uploader (free and easy).  After you create your site this way, you will be able to ‘one-click’ to update your information.

OK, the critical information:

    -Publish to:  FTP server

    -Server address:  wolfweb.unr.edu

    -User name:  your UNR NET ID username

    -Password:  your UNR NETID password

    -Directory / Path:  /homepage/[username]

    -Protocol:  SFTP

    -Port: 22

    -URL:  http://www.unr.edu/~[username]

*You will need a UNR Exchange account (i.e., an email address that ends with “[username]@unr.edu”) to host a website.  If you need one (some grad students), please contact the  Help Desk:

    Phone:  (775) 682-5000

    Email:  help@unr.edu

    *When talking with the Help Desk, request a “Wolfweb account”

    *Once finished, email Christina Clack (cclackATunrDOTed) and she will post it to the EECB website


MAINTENANCE

Having a web presence greatly improves EECB’s visibility--that is unequivocal.  Many faculty and
collaborators spend time surfing the web, looking over other’s research, looking for publications, looking for advisors, etc.  Even me, a nobody-graduate student, had a visitation counter read 561 views from 12 different countries in 14.5 months (right).

One important thing to remember is that websites need regular updates.  If you choose to include a News or Blog page, you should either update it regularly or not have it at all.  It is also important to UPDATE YOUR PUBLICATIONS, even if it is only once per year.  Lastly, a regular, but infrequent task is updating your entire site.  That is, a proverbial gutting.  My site, for instance, I have had for three years and one of my summer to-dos is to completely reformat it because it is looking old.
